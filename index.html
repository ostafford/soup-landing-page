Tour de Dirty 420 Pilgrimage

This tale commences in the harmonious twin towns of Echuca Moama, gracefully at 11.30 am on a delightful Friday. Poised outside the esteemed Dan Murphy's in Echuca, a rented Kia SUV exudes elegance, encircled by bicycles, their doors and boot graciously ajar. On one side of the exquisite vehicle, the first of three riders, Okky, sits atop a resplendent green Prova, meticulously adjusting the connectivity of his petite Bluetooth speaker. On the other side, Aaron, the second rider, bestows his attention on the seat post of his borrowed Baum with utmost finesse. Meanwhile, a lonesome Bastion adorns the forefront of the Kia, as its rider, the gallant Adrian, ventures through the charming main street of Echuca in pursuit of a bike shop, regretting the inadvertent omission of his helmet at home.

The 2018 WNTRSLSTC

Fast forward to the present day - winter 2018, and a lot has changed. Our 2014 project served as the catalyst for jumpstarting our website, while Tourism North East has thrived, creating the kick-ass brand Ride High Country and continuously expanding 7 Peaks. Like long-lost lovers reunited, we now come together for the third running of the Soup Boys #wntrslstc. It's a celebration of the region, a chance to pay our deepest respects and gratitude to the Mitta, Kiewa, and Ovens Valleys - three pockets of the country we genuinely believe are, by and large, the absolute best. This journey that began as a group of enthusiastic adventurers has evolved into something extraordinary, and we couldn't be more grateful for the experiences we've shared.

Creswick Gravel

Adrian crashed within the first 10 minutes, and the ride from Ballarat to Creswick became a memorable struggle, plagued by broken spokes and punctures. It was an unfortunate ordeal that left us hesitant to come back. However, after time has passed and with newfound confidence, we finally return, this time equipped with gravel bikes. Here we are now, a group of six, ready to explore Creswick once more on a brisk Sunday morning, as the Main Street of Creswick slowly awakens. Thankfully, the IGA is open for us, providing a promising start to our new adventure.

Chasing The Dirty 130

We had the wind on our backs leaving Coolamon, lending a much needed helping hand on the return to the finish line in Wagga. Lunch, soft grass to lay upon, and a swim in the river weren't far away. Dead Camel Lane provided a moment of technical concentration that the wider, mostly straight sections hadn't called upon from the days previous. The roots, rocks and ruts climbing up Dead Camel Lane provided enough of a challenge, but the biggest was yet to come – another sit down with Mike Tomalaris, adding enough fire into the bellies of all of us that we were able to push on to the finish line. The end was near.

The Ultimate Guide To: Mount Hotham

Mount Hotham, an imposing climb in Australia, needs little introduction due to its #brand #mythology. It has been featured in various cycling events, playing a significant role in our own cycling journey. For Alex, it was the first-ever climb, and we've experienced it at sunset, as well as during long rides. Early December offered a chance to add another stamp to our 7 Peaks journey. A week after the Tour of Bright, we gathered in Harrietville, a Hamptons-esque* town, for our second ride in this series. Nearly 30 riders joined, many experiencing Hotham for the first time, preparing for their maiden 3 Peaks Challenge. Freshly brewed coffee from Everyday Coffee did little to calm pre-climb nerves.
